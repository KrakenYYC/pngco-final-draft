Ed Kozowy

SASTT A. Sc.T. President

Mr. Kozowy is an Applied Sciences Technologist with a specialization in Petroleum Reservoir Economic Evaluations. Ed graduated from Saskatchewan Polytechnic in Industrial Chemical Technology in 1977, and is a past member/associate of APEGA, APEGS and ASET. His work experience includes Reservoir, Production, Completions Operations and Business Development spanning over 35 years. Ed has held increasing responsibility and positions in the Alberta Research Council, AGAT Laboratories, Dome Petroleum, and Ryder Scott Canada Petroleum Engineers. Mr. Kozowy has served on several public and private energy company boards, and well as several non-profit agencies including past Chair of Family Services Saskatoon Foundation. Mr. Kozowy was the founder and President of Lanex Resources Inc, SunOcean Energy Ltd, and E and H Royalties Inc, and was successful at raising over $12 Million in equity between 2000 and 2010. These companies were directly responsible for staff and consultants while drilling and completing and equipping over twenty-five wells as operator, as well as constructing and operating associated compression and battery facilities, primarily in Alberta, Canada.

David Rose

P.Geol. APEGA - Associate

Mr. Rose is a Professional Geologist who has spent most of his career working at small, Canadian based E&P companies. He brings a diverse set of skills, having robust experience in log analysis, seismic interpretation, and the development of geological, petrophysical and waterflood models. Mr. Rose has extensive operations and A&D experience having drilled upwards of 90 wells, as well as having developed and divested assets, the largest of which was sold for $135M. He graduated from the University of Calgary with a B.Sc in Geology, minoring in Geophysics, and is a member of APEGA.

Kim Wallace

Past Member, APEGA - Associate

Mr. Wallace has been a professional geologist in Western Canada for 43 years. In the first half of his career he worked for various exploration and production companies in both technical and management roles. Then he started, managed and sold a number of successful private oil exploration companies that provided attractive returns to the shareholders. In 2006, he formed a partnership with a progressive First Nations Band in Saskatchewan that created four new reserves using the Treaty Land Entitlement (TLE) process. Through drilling, these newly created reserves proved to have significant oil discovered under them leading to financial independence and a long last relationship with the band. Now Mr. Wallace continues to provide strategic advice to Indigenous Groups in Western Canada participating in oil exploration, green and renewable energy projects, as well as other community projects.
